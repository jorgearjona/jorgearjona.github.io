Bio

Jorge Alberto Arjona, a.k.a. "Churqui", is an instrumental, versatile musician, singer and luthier of Andean wind instruments. Self-taught musician who plays guitar, charango, bass drum, percussion, quena, sikus, among others. Musical Styles: Argentine Folklore - Argentine Tango - Andean Folklore - Latin and International Romantic Melodies, instrumental and sung. Born in San Salvador de Jujuy, Jujuy province, Argentina and living in Hungary for 30 years, after spending a year in Paris, France. Currently in the process of recording material with compositions of his own.

1962/77

Jorge Arjona is born.
Joins "Los del Churcal" band. Jujuy, Argentina.

1980/81

Participate with "Los del Churcal" in the Cosquín Folk Festival, Argentina.

1982/87

"Los del Churcal" with a new formation arrives in La Plata, Argentina, to continue with their music.

1985

International Tour (3 months) in France and Belgium with Ballet Jujeño Huayra Muyoj.

1990

Immigrate to Paris, France. Join the "Altitude" band.

1991/06

Settles in Hungary. He founds the band “Mezcla”.

2000/03

Returns to Paris, France to make summer presentations.

2017

Performs in Austria, Italy and Germany integrating different bands.

2018

Is part of the band "The 3 Latinos" playing in different festivals in Hungary.

2019/20

Founds "J&J Fusion Latino Band", adding children's programs in his musical styles.

Current Projects

Argentine and Andean Folklore Shows, Tango, Latin and International Romantic Melodies (instrumental and sung).

Latin-American and Andean dance musical animation.

Interpretation of Native American Meditation music.

Latin-Hungarian musical production with Júlia Csendes - J&J Fusion Band.

Interactive musical program of stories and legends for children - Júlia and Jorge.
